**Informal**: A function $F : [0,\infty) \times \mathbb{R}^d \to \mathbb{C}$ is positive-definite with respect to the involutive semigroup $([0,\infty) \times \mathbb{R}^d, +)$ with involution $(t,a)^* = (t,-a)$:

## Bochner Representation Theorem (BCR 4.1.13)

### [`semigroupGroupBochner`](../../HilleYosida/SemigroupGroupExtension.lean#L66) — Theorem

**Statement**: If $F : [0,\infty) \times \mathbb{R}^d \to \mathbb{C}$ is bounded, continuous, and positive-definite (in the semigroup sense), then there exists a finite positive measure $\mu$ supported on $[0,\infty) \times \mathbb{R}^d$ such that
$$F(t, a) = \int e^{-tp}\, e^{i\langle a, q \rangle}\, d\mu(p, q) \quad \text{for } t \geq 0.$$

## Group Extension from Bochner Representation

### [`semigroupGroupBochnerExtension`](../../HilleYosida/SemigroupGroupExtension.lean#L116) — Theorem

**Statement**: Given the hypotheses of `semigroupGroupBochner`, there exist a finite positive measure $\mu$ and a function $G : \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^d \to \mathbb{C}$ such that:
1. $F$ has the Laplace representation (for $t \geq 0$)
2. $G(t, a) = \int e^{itp}\, e^{i\langle a, q \rangle}\, d\mu(p, q)$ for all $t \in \mathbb{R}$ (Fourier representation)
3. $G$ is bounded: $\exists\, C,\, \lVert G(t,a) \rVert \leq C$
4. $G$ is continuous on $\mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^d$
5. $G$ is positive-definite on the group $(\mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^d, +)$ with involution $(t,a)^* = (-t,-a)$:
$$\sum_{i,j} \overline{c_i}\, c_j\, G(t_j - t_i,\, a_j - a_i) \geq 0$$

Note: $G$ is **not** a pointwise extension of $F$. They use different kernels ($e^{-tp}$ vs $e^{itp}$) and are related by analytic continuation $t \mapsto -it$.

**Proof uses**: *1 sorry* — follows from `semigroupGroupBochner` + properties of Fourier transforms of finite positive measures. Verified mathematically correct by Gemini Deep Think (2026-03-23).
